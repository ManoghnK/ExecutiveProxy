# Apex Technologies — Human Resources Policy Manual
**Document ID:** HR-POL-001  
**Version:** 3.2  
**Effective Date:** January 1, 2025  
**Owner:** Chief People Officer  
**Classification:** Internal Use Only

---

## 1. Employment & Hiring

### 1.1 Equal Opportunity Employment
Apex Technologies is an equal opportunity employer. All hiring decisions must be based solely on job-related qualifications. Any hiring process that uses demographic data, age, gender, religion, national origin, disability status, or marital status as a filtering criterion is strictly prohibited and constitutes grounds for immediate disciplinary action, up to and including termination.

### 1.2 Background Checks
All full-time offers are contingent on successful completion of a background check through an approved third-party vendor. Background checks must be completed before the employee's first day. Contractors engaged for projects exceeding 90 days are subject to the same requirement. Skipping or expediting background checks without written approval from the CHRO is a policy violation.

### 1.3 Contractor vs. Employee Classification
Workers may not be engaged as independent contractors if they work exclusively for Apex, follow a set schedule dictated by Apex, or use Apex-owned equipment as their primary work tool. Misclassification of employees as contractors exposes the company to IRS penalties and state labor law violations. Any contractor engagement exceeding 12 months must be reviewed by Legal for reclassification risk.

## 2. Compensation & Benefits

### 2.1 Pay Equity
Apex conducts annual pay equity audits across gender and ethnic groups. Managers are prohibited from setting compensation based on a candidate's salary history. All offers must fall within the approved salary band for the role. Exceptions require VP-level approval and written justification filed with HR within 5 business days.

### 2.2 Overtime Policy
Non-exempt employees who work more than 40 hours in a workweek are entitled to overtime pay at 1.5x their regular rate. Managers may not instruct non-exempt employees to work off the clock, skip breaks, or perform work-related tasks outside scheduled hours without compensation. Violations of this policy expose the company to FLSA liability.

### 2.3 Remote Work Stipends
Employees approved for fully remote work are eligible for a one-time home office stipend of $800 and a monthly internet allowance of $50. Stipends are taxable income and will be reported on W-2s. Teams may not create informal stipend arrangements outside this policy.

## 3. Performance Management

### 3.1 Performance Improvement Plans (PIPs)
A PIP must be issued in writing, include specific measurable goals, a timeline of no less than 30 days, and be co-signed by the employee and their manager. Terminating an employee for performance reasons without a prior PIP, unless gross misconduct is involved, creates wrongful termination risk. HR must be notified before any PIP is issued.

### 3.2 Involuntary Termination
All involuntary terminations require HR review and approval before the employee is notified. Legal must be consulted when the employee is over 40 (ADEA risk), has a pending accommodation request, has recently filed an internal complaint, or is part of a group reduction (WARN Act may apply if 50+ employees are affected).

### 3.3 Employee Monitoring
Apex may monitor company-owned devices and corporate network activity for security purposes. Monitoring must be disclosed to employees in their onboarding documentation. Covert monitoring of personal devices, personal email accounts, or physical surveillance of employees outside of company premises is prohibited without a court order. Keystroke logging on personal devices is explicitly prohibited.

## 4. Workplace Conduct

### 4.1 Anti-Harassment Policy
Harassment of any kind — verbal, physical, visual, or digital — based on any protected characteristic is prohibited. This includes conduct by employees, contractors, vendors, and clients. All complaints must be investigated within 10 business days. Retaliation against a complainant is a separate and equally serious violation.

### 4.2 Conflicts of Interest
Employees must disclose any financial interest in a vendor, competitor, or customer to their manager and HR. Employees may not approve contracts with vendors in which they hold a financial interest. Violations are subject to clawback of any compensation earned during the conflict period.

### 4.3 Social Media Policy
Employees may not post confidential company information, unreleased product details, financial data, or disparaging content about colleagues, clients, or partners on personal social media. Posts that could be construed as official company positions require prior approval from Communications.

## 5. Leave Policies

### 5.1 Paid Time Off
Full-time employees accrue PTO at a rate of 1.67 days per month (20 days annually). PTO does not roll over beyond 30 days. Managers may not deny PTO requests for employees who have provided 5 or more business days of notice except in documented business-critical situations. Denial must be documented in writing.

### 5.2 Parental Leave
Primary caregivers are entitled to 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave. Secondary caregivers are entitled to 6 weeks. Leave may begin up to 4 weeks before the expected birth or placement date. Requiring an employee to return early from parental leave or penalizing them in performance reviews for taking leave is a violation of federal and state law.

### 5.3 Medical & FMLA Leave
Employees who have worked for Apex for at least 12 months and 1,250 hours are eligible for up to 12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected FMLA leave per year. Apex will maintain health insurance during FMLA leave. Terminating or demoting an employee while on FMLA leave is an FMLA interference violation.

---

## 6. Workforce Changes

### 6.1 Layoffs and Reductions in Force
Any reduction in force affecting 50 or more employees at a single site within a 30-day period requires 60 days advance written notice under the WARN Act. Failure to comply exposes the company to liability equal to 60 days of back pay and benefits per affected employee. Legal must be engaged at least 90 days before any planned RIF.

### 6.2 Offshoring and Role Transfers
Transferring roles or functions to international third-party vendors (offshoring) does not automatically constitute a layoff, but requires HR and Legal review when 10 or more domestic positions are affected. Any offshoring arrangement involving access to PII of US residents must be reviewed by the Data Privacy team for cross-border data transfer compliance.

### 6.3 Severance
Severance is not legally required but is company policy for involuntary terminations without cause. Standard severance is 2 weeks per year of service, up to a maximum of 26 weeks. Severance agreements must include a release of claims and be reviewed by Legal before distribution. Employees over 40 must be given 21 days to consider and 7 days to revoke a severance agreement (OWBPA requirement).
