## 1. Data Privacy & Protection

### 1.1 GDPR Compliance
Apex Technologies processes personal data of EU/EEA residents and is subject to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). Any new product feature, internal tool, or third-party integration that collects, stores, or processes personal data of EU residents must undergo a Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) before launch. Shipping a feature that processes EU personal data without a completed DPIA is a regulatory violation that can result in fines up to 4% of global annual revenue.

### 1.2 CCPA Compliance
California residents have the right to know what personal data is collected about them, request deletion, and opt out of sale of their data. Any new data collection touchpoint targeting California users must be reviewed by the Privacy team before deployment. "Selling" data under CCPA includes sharing data with third parties for cross-context behavioral advertising, even without direct payment.

### 1.3 Data Minimization
Apex follows a data minimization principle: collect only the data necessary for the stated purpose. Engineering teams may not add additional data collection fields to user-facing products without Privacy team approval. Retaining data beyond its stated retention period without a documented legal hold is a compliance violation.

### 1.4 Cross-Border Data Transfers
Transferring personal data of EU residents outside the EEA requires a valid legal mechanism: Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs), Binding Corporate Rules, or an adequacy decision. Transferring EU personal data to a vendor or offshore team without SCCs in place is an immediate GDPR violation. The Legal team maintains a register of approved cross-border transfer mechanisms.

### 1.5 Data Breach Notification
A confirmed breach of personal data must be reported to the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of discovery under GDPR. US state breach notification laws require notification to affected individuals within 30-90 days depending on jurisdiction. The Security team must be notified within 1 hour of suspected breach discovery. Delaying breach notification to manage PR is a regulatory violation.

## 2. Intellectual Property

### 2.1 Open Source Usage
All open source libraries used in Apex products must be registered in the approved OSS inventory. Copyleft licenses (GPL, AGPL, LGPL) require Legal review before use in any commercial product — use of AGPL-licensed code in a SaaS product may require open-sourcing the entire product codebase. Engineers must not introduce copyleft-licensed dependencies into production code without written Legal approval.

### 2.2 Third-Party Code and AI-Generated Code
Code generated by AI tools (GitHub Copilot, ChatGPT, etc.) may incorporate copyrighted training data. Engineers must not use AI-generated code verbatim in production for any module that handles core IP, cryptography, or security-sensitive operations. Legal review is required before shipping AI-generated code in regulated product areas.

### 2.3 Patent Considerations
Before launching a feature that implements a novel algorithm or technical method, the team lead must submit a brief invention disclosure to Legal. Apex holds 23 active patents and 11 pending applications. Shipping without disclosure review creates freedom-to-operate risk and forfeits potential patent protection.

### 2.4 Non-Disclosure Agreements
All vendor engagements, partnership discussions, and beta customer relationships must be covered by a signed NDA before confidential information is shared. Using a vendor's standard NDA without Legal review is prohibited — Apex's standard mutual NDA must be used or the vendor's NDA must be redlined by Legal.

## 3. Financial & Securities Compliance

### 3.1 Material Non-Public Information (MNPI)
Employees with access to unreleased financial results, M&A activity, or major contract wins are considered insiders. Trading Apex stock or tipping others to trade while in possession of MNPI is insider trading — a federal crime. The blackout period for all employees runs from 2 weeks before quarter-end through 48 hours after the quarterly earnings release.

### 3.2 Expense Reporting
All business expenses must be submitted within 30 days of occurrence with original receipts. Expenses over $500 require manager pre-approval. Entertainment expenses involving government officials must be reviewed by Legal regardless of amount — even a business meal may implicate the Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA) in certain jurisdictions.

### 3.3 Revenue Recognition
Apex follows ASC 606 for revenue recognition. Engineering and Sales may not make commitments to customers that modify the delivery timeline, acceptance criteria, or payment terms of a contract without Finance sign-off. Informal commitments that pull revenue into an earlier quarter are a securities compliance risk.

## 4. Employment Law

### 4.1 Non-Compete Agreements
Non-compete agreements are unenforceable in California, North Dakota, and Minnesota, and subject to strict limitations in most other states following the FTC's 2024 rulemaking. Apex no longer uses non-compete clauses for individual contributors. Senior executives (VP and above) may be subject to a 12-month non-solicitation agreement reviewed and approved by Legal.

### 4.2 Wage and Hour Compliance
Misclassifying employees as exempt from overtime when they do not meet the duties test (executive, administrative, or professional exemption) exposes Apex to multi-year back pay liability. Any job redesign that changes an employee's primary duties must be reviewed by HR and Legal to confirm exemption status.

### 4.3 International Employment
Hiring employees in new jurisdictions requires establishing a legal entity or using an Employer of Record (EOR) service. Paying foreign nationals as independent contractors when they function as employees violates local labor law in most jurisdictions and creates permanent establishment tax risk. Legal must approve all new-country hiring before any offer is extended.

## 5. Vendor & Third-Party Risk

### 5.1 Vendor Due Diligence
All new vendors with access to Apex systems, data, or customer information must complete the Vendor Risk Assessment before contract execution. Vendors handling PII must sign a Data Processing Agreement (DPA). Skipping vendor due diligence to accelerate a project timeline is not an acceptable risk trade-off.

### 5.2 Subprocessors
If a vendor subcontracts any portion of their service to a third party that will access Apex data, that subprocessor must also be approved via the vendor risk process. Vendors must notify Apex of new subprocessors with 30 days advance notice.

### 5.3 Sanctions Compliance
Apex may not engage vendors, partners, or customers that appear on OFAC's Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) list or are domiciled in sanctioned countries. All new vendor relationships must be screened against the SDN list before contract execution. This applies regardless of contract value.
